Edward Snowden: Whistleblower or Traitor?

"The truth will always find a way out."

0 days since the first NSA leak — June 5, 2013

Who is Edward Snowden?

Edward Snowden is a former contractor for the National Security Agency (NSA) who became widely known in 2013 for leaking documents about global surveillance programs. After the disclosures, he fled the United States and was eventually granted asylum in Russia, where he currently resides.

I don't want to live in a world where everything I do and say is recorded. That is not something I am willing to support or live under.

— Edward Snowden, 2013

Snowden's Claims

Snowden argued that the surveillance programs were overly aggressive and lacked transparency. His goal was not to harm national security but to inform the public so that they could amend the laws in a democratic society.

The Government's Position

The U.S. government maintains that Snowden's actions were illegal and harmful to national security. Officials argue the disclosures endangered lives and sources, and that Snowden bypassed legal channels entirely.
